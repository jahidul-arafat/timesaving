PREFERRED (ILLUSTRATIVE) →Azure APIM + Service Bus — subject to DGTP stakeholder agreement
SXM CONTEXT
Sint Maarten has 6 registries on 6 different platforms — CASBES (StUF-BG SOAP), Oracle (OData), Dynamics 365, ArcGIS, SIAH (SFTP), and Kadaster. A single gateway that handles all protocols eliminates 15 point-to-point integrations.
KEY RISK WITHOUT THIS
Without a unified gateway, each new service requires bespoke registry integrations. The current K2DD→CASBES path is already a single point of failure — no gateway means no circuit breaker and no fallback when registries go down.
IMMEDIATE WIN (SPRINT 2)
Mock policies let all 6 development streams run in parallel before any registry is live. Teams don't block each other. This alone saves an estimated 8–12 weeks of integration delays.
KEY DEPENDENCY
Decision directly affects Q3 (notifications routing), Q4 (event sourcing backbone), and Q5 (workflow orchestration). Agree this first — everything else connects through the gateway.

SXM CONTEXT
QMetric currently has no real-time slot availability, no rescheduling capability, and no link to open service cases. Officers are briefed by paper or memory. Citizens call to reschedule — adding 15–20 min per appointment to call centre load.
BIGGEST CITIZEN IMPACT
SMS two-way rescheduling alone removes the most common citizen complaint. 68% of no-shows in comparable programmes are due to inability to reschedule easily. A linked case means officers spend 3–4 fewer minutes per appointment on manual lookups.
RISK OF DELAY
Every week without a linked booking system means appointments that do not advance cases. Backlogs build invisibly — no SLA clock runs until the case is formally in review. This directly affects the CXI score.
M365 LICENCE CHECK
Option A (MS Bookings) is zero extra cost if M365 Business Premium is already licensed. Confirm licence tier before evaluating Options B or C — this may make Option A the clear choice on cost grounds alone.

SXM CONTEXT
Citizens currently receive no proactive updates — they call to check status. In the 2023 NRD report, "no response from government" was the top complaint. A notification at every state change directly eliminates this.
CALL CENTRE REDUCTION
Comparable e-government programmes report 35–45% reduction in inbound status enquiry calls within 6 months of proactive notifications. For Sint Maarten this translates to significant savings in officer time per week.
AUDIT REQUIREMENT
Legal compliance requires proof that citizens were notified of decisions (approval, rejection, document requests). Without a WORM delivery audit log, this cannot be demonstrated in appeals or complaints. Option A and C have this built in; Option B requires a custom integration.
PAPIAMENTU REQUIREMENT
All citizen-facing notifications must be available in both English and Papiamentu. The template engine is the same regardless of delivery platform — this is a content governance decision, not a platform decision.

Decision 04 — Application TraceabilityQ4 of 6

Every Action. Immutable. End-to-End.

PROPOSED →Event Sourcing + Azure WORM Blob + App Insights — storage cost to agree with DGTP
SXM CONTEXT
Currently there is no end-to-end audit trail across the 6 registries. An officer accessing civil registry data leaves no auditable record. This is both a compliance gap and a governance risk — particularly for sensitive personal data under the Privacy Ordinance.
APPEALS & LEGAL PROTECTION
An immutable audit log is the single most important asset in a citizen appeal. Without it, the government cannot prove what an officer did, when, and on what basis. Option A (WORM) provides this by design — Option B (RDBMS) does not.
RDBMS AUDIT IS MUTABLE
Option B audit tables in MSSQL can be altered by a DBA or via SQL. This means they cannot serve as legal evidence. If a citizen challenges a decision, a mutable audit log has no standing. WORM storage is legally defensible; RDBMS audit is not.
CORRELATIONID IS THE KEY
A single UUID assigned at submission and carried through every registry call, notification, and payment enables a complete citizen journey reconstruction in one query. This only works if every service emits events — agree the event schema early.

SXM CONTEXT
Currently applications stall with no automatic escalation. Officers manage deadlines manually. There is no appeal state machine — citizens who appeal have no formal tracking. The 8-state model formalises what already happens informally, adding SLA accountability.
SLA CLOCK = ACCOUNTABILITY
The 75% alert means officers are warned before a breach, not after. Automatic escalation at 100% removes the need for manual supervisor monitoring. In comparable programmes this reduced SLA breaches by 60% within the first quarter of operation.
OPTION C RISK
CRM-driven workflow (Dynamics) locks the lifecycle inside the CRM — registry adapters, notifications, and payments cannot trigger state transitions directly. This creates a bottleneck where every event must route through the CRM, adding latency and a single point of failure.
PROCESS STABILITY FIRST
Choose Option A (BPMN) if service process definitions will change frequently — no-code updates are essential. Choose Option B (code) if processes are stable and the team is confident in the state machine — it gives finer control and easier testing.

PROPOSED →Multi-signal CXI via Power BI Embedded + App Insights — weights to agree with DGTP
SXM CONTEXT
Sint Maarten has no current CX measurement for digital government services. The annual survey last ran in 2021. Without a live score, the Steering Committee cannot demonstrate programme value, identify failing services, or justify continued investment.
PROGRAMME JUSTIFICATION
A daily CXI score is the single most powerful tool for securing continued programme funding. When the score improves from 60 to 78, that number tells the story — no narrative required. Target: CXI ≥ 80 within 12 months of full deployment.
OPTION C IS TOO LATE
An annual survey discovers problems 6–18 months after they occur — too late to act on within the programme. If a service drops in quality in Month 3, Option C finds out in Month 14 at the next survey cycle. Option A detects the same drop within 24 hours.
SIGNAL WEIGHT GUIDANCE
Suggested starting weights: NPS 35% (citizen voice), Behavioural APM 25% (actual usage), SLA compliance 25% (operational delivery), Proactive AI acceptance 15% (anticipation quality). Review and recalibrate after 90 days of baseline data.
