EXECUTIVE SUMMARY · 30-SECOND READ02 / 12

What this RFP is, in plain English

Saint Lucia's Ministry of Education wants a single firm to design, build, host, train, and hand over a national EMIS — primary and secondary schools — in 24 months on a fixed-price contract, then walk away leaving the Ministry fully self-sufficient.

SCOPE
Two Phases of a Three-Phase Program
Phase 1 (weeks 1–52): foundational EMIS setup. Phase 2 (weeks 53–104): core operations + handover. Phase 3 ("Integrated Services") is a separate procurement — not in this contract.
2 OF 3 PHASES
COMMERCIAL
Lump-Sum, No Inflation Adjustment
Fixed price for fixed scope, milestone-gated payments. No price adjustment for inflation/FX in 24 months. 25% withholding tax. 10% advance with bank guarantee. Margin compression risk on us.
FIXED-PRICE RISK
EXIT
Total Handover at Week 104
Source code transfer, IP rights assignment, hosting environment hand-back, full SOPs, technical KT. Client explicitly wants to "minimize vendor lock-in through open and modular solutions." This is THE win theme.

★ WHAT THE CLIENT REALLY WANTS
Sustainable Ministry independence after handover. Past EMIS pilots failed on scalability, usability, historical retention, and integration — fix all four explicitly. Caribbean / SIDS context fluency matters (CXC assessments, EC dollars, hurricane DR).
⚡ TOP-3 RISKS TO US

MoE must deliver: SRS, Business Requirements, EMIS Open Data Standard, sample data, focal points, access to all 10 named systems, and approved EMIS Policy with data governance. Any delay cascades into our fixed-price timeline.
GO / NO-GO DECISION03 / 12

Should we bid?

A balanced view — what makes this RFP attractive, what makes it dangerous, and our recommendation. The decision is reversible up to the submission deadline; the cost of a serious bid is real.

REASONS TO PURSUE
Stable, well-funded client
World Bank–financed under USD $25.6M CARDTP envelope. Payment risk is minimal once milestones are accepted. No client-credit underwriting concern.
Bounded scale, single jurisdiction
~75 public schools, ~25–30k students. Manageable footprint relative to the 24-month window. No multi-tenancy complexity.
Reusable platform asset
Outputs can be repackaged for the four other OECS countries also under CARDTP. Builds an OECS regional reference; potentially fundable through the same World Bank instrument.
Clear evaluation criteria
QCBS scoring is published in the Data Sheet. We know exactly where points come from. No black-box subjectivity.
REASONS FOR CAUTION
Lump-sum, no inflation adjustment
24 months of inflation, FX drift, and global tech price changes are entirely on us. Insurance ≥ contract ceiling adds material cost. Build at least 8–12% contingency.
Integration discovery risk
10 named systems. Some are an "Excel database" (HRM) and a "MySQL/PHP" Kindergarten Portal. Documented APIs are not guaranteed. Underestimate exposure: 15–20% of effort.
Mandatory IP & source-code transfer
Any "Background IP" we embed becomes perpetually licensed to the Ministry. Effectively rules out using a proprietary commercial EMIS as the base. Open-source baseline is forced.
Phase 3 follow-on not guaranteed
A competitor could win Phase 3 using the platform we built. We cannot price the bid assuming Phase 3 revenue.
RECOMMENDATION PURSUE — with two conditions: (1) a Saint Lucia / OECS local partner is secured for the 5-point "Participation by Nationals" criterion, and (2) we receive answers to the 8 critical clarifications (slide 11) by 15 April 2026. Without either, defer.

TARGET ARCHITECTURE05 / 12

Reference architecture for the proposal

Modular monolith + PWA front-end, open-source stack, cloud-portable. Designed to satisfy offline-first, WCAG 2.1, RBAC, audit, and — critically — Ministry hand-back at week 104 with zero vendor lock-in.

REFERENCE ARCHITECTURE — HOVER EACH NODE FOR DETAILS
DESIGN PRINCIPLES — IN PRIORITY ORDER
1. Zero Vendor Lock-In RFP §9.1 explicit ▾
Every component must be open-source or have a perpetual, royalty-free license to the Ministry. PostgreSQL, Keycloak, Kong, Metabase/Superset, Airflow, Zammad. Cloud-managed services only where they are commodity (S3-compatible storage, managed K8s) and re-portable. This is the #1 win theme — lead with it.
2. Offline-First Is Architectural All phases · §7 Offline ▾
PWA, encrypted IndexedDB + SQLite-WASM, service-worker sync, conflict resolution (timestamp + admin override + user-confirmed UI), offline auth with cached credentials and lockout, offline reports as PDF + cached HTML, offline backup with password-protected JSON/CSV. Validate sync engine in inception spike.
3. Layered + Modular (not microservices) §7 Tech Reqs ▾
Modular monolith with bounded contexts (Institution, Personnel, Student, Census, Notification, Audit). Microservices are wrong here — Ministry must operate this with a small team after handover. Layered architecture (data / business / presentation) per §7 mandate. Service-oriented APIs internally and externally.
4. Security & Privacy by Design SCC 20.1 + RFP ▾
Encryption at rest (AES-256), in transit (TLS 1.2+), and on-edge (encrypted local DB). RBAC, MFA, audit log, DPIA. PII inventory. Cyber controls per SCC 20.1. WCAG 2.1 AA conformance. Data classification scheme. Incident response runbook. Tested DR (climate-resilience matters in the Caribbean).
OPEN QUESTION — TO CLARIFY BEFORE 15 APR
"Hosting transfer to Ministry" — cloud account hand-off or physical migration?
If cloud-account hand-off: we use AWS/Azure managed services freely and re-assign account ownership at handover. If physical migration: we must avoid all cloud-managed services and use only what runs in a Ministry datacenter. The architecture differs by 30%.

FINAL SCORE FORMULA & LEVERAGE
S = 0.80 × Technical + 0.20 × Financial
Financial score: Sf = 100 × (Lowest Price ÷ Your Price). Underbidding by 10% relative to the lowest competitor buys you ~2 final points. Outperforming on technical by even 5 points buys you 4 final points — 2× the leverage of price.

⚡ DO NOT BLOW THE FLOOR

RECOMMENDATION & DECISION12 / 12

Pursue — with two conditions

A clear path to a winnable bid, provided we move on partner selection this week and clarifications by 15 April. Here is what we are asking for from leadership today.

Pursue this RFP.
